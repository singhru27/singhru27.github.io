Personas - Improving Household Appliances

The Problem

What makes a interface "tick"? Even the most common devices - those that we take completely for granted - rely on effective interfaces to function properly.

I set out to conduct a user analysis for on of the most ubiquitous interfaces used today: the AC control unit. I wanted to find out the types of users that use this interface, and to glean out the major problems faced by those users.

In addition, I had the goal of creating a definitive set of user personas for the AC unit. This would allow future designers to pinpoint the exact needs of users as they created new iterations of this interface.

For this project, I observed usage of a control panel for an AC unit in private video gaming/boarding gaming rooms at a community recreation center. This unit experiences heavy usage and is the perfect target for conducting a user analysis study.

User Research/User Needs

To research this interface, I interviewed three individuals - individuals from all walks of life - to discover what they liked, loved and hated. The distilled and summarized responses are presented below.
Interview Questions and Answers
Observations
  1. When is your birthdate?
    Individuals ranged in age from 15 to 50 years old.
  2. What drove you to interact with the control panel interface?
    The older users interacted with the interface at his kids' request. The other two wanted a colder room.
  3. Do you feel that there are features that every ideal AC control interface should have?
    All three individuals responded yes
  4. (Asked only if previous was yes) What would these be?
    Common requests included a power button, temperature controls, and "automatic mode" button for automatically regulating temperature. One user requested programmability.
  5. What was your first reaction to the control panel interface?
    All three individuals stated that the item was too old and manual.
  6. On a scale of 1-10, with 1 being simplest and 10 being most difficult, how difficult did you find the control panel to use?
    The father of two rated the device an 8, the teenager rated it an 8, the graduate student rated it a 6.
  7. True or False; There were aspects of the control panel that were challenging or confusing to use.
    All answered True except the middle aged father of two.
  8. (Asked only if the prior question was answered True): What aspect or aspects of the control panel did you find challenging?
    The graduate student and teenager both mentioned that it was difficult to discern the purpose of the "Mode" and "Fan" buttons. The graduate student complained that the lights were too dim and the buttons were too hard to press.
  9. Have you used wall-mounted AC units before?
    All responded Yes, except for the teenager.
  10. (Asked only if the prior question was answered True): Have you used wall-mounted AC units that allow you to adjust both fan intensity as well as temperature? If you don’t remember, let me know
    Both users who answered Yes to the previous question answered Yes.
  1. Users tended to have difficulty pressing the buttons on the device. Multiple users had to press the button multiple times before the device did what they were trying to get it to do.

  2. The middle-aged parent was able to complete the task in the shortest amount of time. He showed little hesitation as he set the appropriate settings in under 15 seconds.

  3. The teenage user resorted to using the light on her phone to illuminate the screen.

  4. The teenage user repeatedly pressed the same sequence of buttons multiple times, and checked the effect that it had on the AC unit.

  5. The user pressed the “Fan” button twice to set the fan to the lowest level. He pressed the buttons with noticeably more force, resulting in a successful push on the first try. He then pressed the “down” button twice to lower the temperature.

  6. The graduate student first tried to hold down the "Down" button to decrease the temperature. After the temperature only decreased by 1 degree, the user released the button, let out a small sigh, and manually repeatedly pushed the “down” button until the temperature was set at the max low level. She was pressing the button too fast for the interface to respond to each press, and so only about every second press was actually registering (and causing the temperature to adjust accordingly)

  7. The graduate student did not have to use her light to illuminate the buttons, but she did cup her hands over the dimly lit display and squinted obviously.

Creating the Empathy Maps/Personas

Armed with this knowledge, I set out to create two representative personas.

Persona 1

User Description

Frazzled Frank is a middle-aged stay-at-home father of two.

He has two rambunctious young children, with whom he spends much of his time with and frequently takes out for activities at the local recreation center and other kid-friendly locations.

Interface Problems

Frank finds the interface very manual and tedious to use. He finds that he must step through multiple steps to accomplish his objectives, and finds doing so difficult while taking care of his children.

Frank finds the buttons on the interface hard to press, as they are manual (not capacitive) buttons that require a fair amount of force.

Frank finds it annoying that the buttons on the interface must be repeatedly pressed to increment the settings (i.e changing the temperature by 5 degrees requires 5 separate button pushes).

Frank has a bit of trouble reading the small font sizes on the controls of the device and viewing the light that reflects user selections.

User Selection Explanation

According to Census.gov, 61.6% of men in America are fathers, and about 50% classify as middle-aged (early thirties to early sixties) representing a very large user base for the product.

Frank was chosen as a persona as he encapsulates a very large slice of users who all exhibit similar behavior drawn upon similar life experiences and similar familial dynamics.

Frank represents the classic archetype of a middle-aged individual in America. He has experience with and is fully capable of using older interfaces (as these individuals have lived through and used technology of the last several decades), but much prefers the ease of use associated with newer designs.

Frank exhibits this familiarity when he quickly and efficiently tunes the AC to fit his needs.

Frank represents users who are busy, distracted, and prefer quick-response interfaces that can adapt quickly using the latest tools available.

Persona 2

User Description

Amy is a 16 year old female, has two siblings, has many friends, and is currently finishing up high school.

Amy grew up in the age of technology, and is familiar with the newest innovations. She doesn’t have a ton of responsibilities on her plate, and enjoys hanging out with her friends (especially at her local community recreation center).

Interface Problems

Amy finds the interface very manual and unintuitive. She has difficulty understanding how all the settings tie together, and how they should be used to achieve her desired temperature.

Amy has a hard time pressing the buttons, and finds them clunky and unresponsive to her actions. She also finds the coloring distasteful.

Amy doesn’t like how the buttons require separate pushes to increment (i.e, one needs to press the “down” button five separate times to adjust the temperature down by 5 degrees).

Amy has a difficult time reading the light that indicates what setting the unit is on, especially in the brightly lit room.

Amy wishes the interface did not require so much close-up interaction, and could be controlled from farther away.

User Selection Explanation

According to Statista, Gen Z (those born in the mid 90’s to mid 2000’s) make up 21% of the population.

These individuals represent the next generation of frequent UI users, and interfaces must able to satisfy their needs to be viable.

Amy represents the archetypal Gen Z individual; She is very well-versed in technology, has an innate understanding of how the newest touch and voice based UIs work, and has spent nearly the entirety of her life in the midst of the technological revolution.

Amy has a somewhat limited understanding of dated technology, and finds UI’s based on concepts from earlier decades a bit more difficult to use given her lack of experience.

Amy has a larger social network, and is often found in communal places where this AC interface is located in.

Storyboarding

As a final step, I created a storyboard to show the sequential interaction pattern of "new-age Amy" with this interface

Amy enters the recreation center, followed by her friends, and spots the AC unit.

Feeling very hot, Amy approaches the AC unit and removes the protective covering in hopes of adjusting the room temperature.

Amy presses the power button twice (the second press harder than the first as the first press did not register) to power up the unit.

Amy glances back to tell her friends she understands how to use the AC unit, while putting her hands over the unit to check airflow.

Amy removes her touch-screen phone from her pocket, and illuminates the AC unit interface using the built-in light.

Amy cups her hands over the lights to see better, and switches the Mode setting to fan.

Amy confusedly adjusts the fan level as well, trying to figure out how the two interact.

Amy places her hands over the vent to evaluate the airflow, and to attempt to determine what impact her previous actions had on the underlying device.

Amy switches the Mode Setting back to Cool, in order to switch the unit back to air conditioning mode.

Amy attempts to reduce the temperature by holding down the button, but fails to do so (due to the fact that a single button push can only increment by one degree each) and squints her eyes.

Amy manually presses the button to reduce temperature, exhibiting frustration while doing so.

Amy evaluates airflow one last time, then walks away from the AC interface.

Conclusion/Parting Thoughts

Users tend to crave automation and automatic features in appliances. It appears that even older individuals have learned how to use the newer technologies, and actively seek out these technologies that reduce interface usage time.

Resistive style touch buttons tend to be uniformly disliked by users. Users have grown accustomed to the capacitive touch screen style, and oftentimes will not press hard enough on older interfaces to elicit a response.

I found very surprising the amount of overlap in usage patterns between individuals with large age-gaps. Even with vastly different backgrounds and experiences, there are many commonalities in the features that users actively seek out.

What we anticipate will be problems with real users often doesn't match up with reality. It's really important to actually carry out interviews and real-world observations to get an unbiased understanding of what works and what doesn't.

Overall, I really enjoyed this project. I gained a new-found appreciation for the persona-creation and storyboarding process, and look forward to applying these skills towards upcoming design challenges.
